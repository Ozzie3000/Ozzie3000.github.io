The Library
of Alexandria

after the story by Jorge Luis Borges, 1941

An endless honeycomb of hexagonal galleries, each wall bearing five shelves, each shelf thirty-five volumes, every volume four hundred ten pages of twenty-five symbols. Every book that can exist is here, once, somewhere. Take volumes from the shelves and read them. Or name any passage you wish existed — the library will tell you exactly where it has always been, and carry you to it.

Seek a text

The library knows twenty-two letters, the space, the comma and the period. Other characters will be translated into the nearest symbol, or fall away. Whatever remains exists, already, on some page — the library will reveal where.
